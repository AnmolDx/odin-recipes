Briyani

Discription

Biryani is one of the most popular dishes in South Asia and among the South Asian diaspora. The dish is often associated with the region's Muslim population. Similar dishes are prepared in many other countries, often with local variations, and often brought there by South Asian diaspora populations. Biryani is the most-ordered dish on Indian online food ordering and delivery services, is used in weddings and celebrations throughout the region, and has been described as the most popular dish in India.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chicken: Bone-in, curry cut
  • 3 cups Basmati Rice: Soaked for 30 mins
  • 4 Large Onions: Sliced and deep-fried until golden (Birista)
  • Marinade: 1 cup yogurt, 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tbsp garam masala, 1 tsp red chili powder, Â½ tsp turmeric, salt to taste
  • Whole Spices: Bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick
  • Garnish: Fresh mint, coriander, saffron soaked in warm milk, and 4 tbsp ghee

Steps

  1. Marinate: Mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and powdered spices. Let rest for at least 1 hour.
  2. Par-boil Rice: Boil water with salt and whole spices. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (still slightly hard). Drain fully.
  3. Cook Base: In a heavy pot, sear the marinated chicken in oil/ghee for 5-7 minutes until partially cooked.
  4. Layer: Spread the 70% cooked rice over the chicken. Top with fried onions, fresh herbs, saffron milk, and melted ghee.
  5. Dum (Steam): Cover pot tightly with a lid (seal with foil if needed). Cook on very low heat for 20 minutes.
  6. Serve: Let it rest for 10 minutes, then fluff gently to mix the layers.
